# Electrical Architecture & FPGA Control Logic

## 1. Pulse Forming Network (PFN)
Gundam Robotics Systems bypasses standard millisecond-delayed PLCs, deploying a high-speed Field Programmable Gate Array (FPGA) to orchestrate power delivery across independent high-voltage rails.

## 2. Timing Sequencing ($5\mu\text{s}$ Cycle)
To optimize the Maxwell-force ejection, the FPGA must execute gate signals with nanosecond-level precision. The VHDL-implemented state machine operates on a 50MHz internal clock (20ns tick cycles):

1. **T+0 $\mu\text{s}$ (Tick 0):** High-Amplitude UV Rail is energized via high-speed Silicon Carbide (SiC) MOSFET gate drivers, saturating the chamber and inducing the solid-plasma transition.
2. **T+1 $\mu\text{s}$ (Tick 50):** The High-Current Pulse Rail is energized via an IGBT module, driving the 4-layer Bitter stack to generate peak magnetic flux precisely as air density reaches maximum ionization.
3. **T+5 $\mu\text{s}$ (Tick 250):** Both UV and Magnetic gate pulses are instantly terminated, concluding the linear force ejection stroke.
4. **Cooldown Phase:** The 48V Vane-Axial cooling fan system remains actively latched to rapidly dissipate mechanical heat and purge internal moisture.

## 3. Circuit Isolation & Fail-Safes
* **Opto-Isolators:** Opto-couplers are integrated into the gate output tracks to completely isolate the sensitive FPGA clock pins from massive back-EMF spikes generated by the 240W inductive load.
* **Flyback Protection:** Ultra-fast recovery diodes are wired in anti-parallel to the Bitter stack to clamp inductive voltage spikes.
* **Hardware Interrupts:** Back-EMF monitoring maps the physical piston path, triggering a soft limit slow-down command at 95 mm, and an unconditional power cut at 100 mm.
