## 1. System Overview

The `bak_flight_controller.py` script serves as the critical bridge between conventional aerodynamic flight telemetry and the Type-S electrostatic propulsion array. 

Traditional aircraft utilize mechanical control surfaces (ailerons, elevators, rudders) to alter fluid dynamics. The Type-S platform utilizes an **Electrostatic Displacement System**. This controller parses standard flight intent (Pitch, Roll, Yaw, Throttle) and translates it into a 360-degree high-voltage charge distribution matrix. 

By dynamically varying the Coulomb saturation across specific quadrants of the 360-plate array, the controller creates localized asymmetric repulsion vectors against the Earth's electric field, enabling omnidirectional maneuverability.

## 2. Mathematical Model

The flight controller calculates the necessary charge distribution in real-time based on the following physical constants and equations:

* **Vessel Mass (m):** 1,000 kg
* **Gravity (g):** 9.81 m/s^2
* **Earth Surface E-Field (E):** ~100 N/C

### 2.1 Base Hover Charge (Throttle)
To counteract gravity, the entire ventral hull must maintain a baseline static charge capable of producing an upward repulsive force equal to the vessel's weight.
`q_total = (m * g) / E = 98.1 Coulombs`

The `throttle` input (0.0 to 2.0) acts as a multiplier against this base charge. A throttle of 1.0 maintains a perfect hover. A throttle of >1.0 induces vertical ascent.

### 2.2 Asymmetric Bias (Pitch and Roll)
To translate horizontally or initiate turns, the controller applies a sinusoidal and cosinusoidal bias to the charge distribution around the 360-degree array.

* **Pitch (Forward/Backward Translation):** Controlled via Cosine. A negative pitch (nose down) reduces charge on the front plates (Index 0) and increases charge on the rear plates (Index 180), creating a forward pushing vector.
* **Roll (Lateral Translation):** Controlled via Sine. A positive roll (right wing up) increases charge on the right plates (Index 90) and decreases charge on the left plates (Index 270), creating a leftward lateral slide.
